Welcome

This guide teaches natural numbers and their basic operations in simple, plain English. If a term is new, we define it right away. Short chunks. Clear examples. No fluff.

Natural Numbers

  • We count objects using numbers: 1, 2, 3, …
  • In this course, 0 is included in the natural numbers: \(\mathbb{N} = \{0,1,2,\dots\}\).
  • We use \(\mathbb{N}\) (or sometimes \(\mathbb{N}_0\)) to denote this set.

Integers

  • Subtraction may lead below 0. We extend \(\mathbb{N}\) with negatives to get the integers.
  • \(\mathbb{Z} = \{\ldots,-3,-2,-1,0,1,2,3,\ldots\}\) extends in both directions forever.

Operations

Addition and multiplication of natural numbers stay within \(\mathbb{N}\).

Subtraction and division can fall outside \(\mathbb{N}\).

Multiplication is repeated addition: \(m\times n = m + m + \cdots + m\) (\(n\) times).

Exponentiation is repeated multiplication: \(m^k = m \times m \times \cdots \times m\) (\(k\) times).

Sign rules: \((-m)\times n = -(m\cdot n)\), and \((-m)\times(-n)=m\cdot n\).

Division & Remainder

Division is repeated subtraction. For integers \(a\) and \(b>0\), we can write \(a = bq + r\) with \(0 \le r < b\). Here, \(q\) is the quotient and \(r\) is the remainder. We write \(a\bmod b = r\).

Factors & Primes

  • Divides: \(a\mid b\) if \(b\bmod a = 0\). Then, \(b\) is a multiple of \(a\).
  • Factors come in pairs (e.g., factors of 12: \(\{1,12\},\{2,6\},\{3,4\}\)).
  • Prime: a number with exactly two factors, 1 and itself. 1 is not prime. 2 is the only even prime.
  • Every integer \(>1\) has a unique prime factorization.
