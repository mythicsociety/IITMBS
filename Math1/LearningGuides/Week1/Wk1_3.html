Real and Complex Numbers

We've seen that rational numbers (fractions) are "dense," meaning you can always find one between any two others. So, do they fill up the entire number line completely? Let's find out.

Numbers That Aren't Fractions

Let's think about square roots. The square root of 9 is 3, which is a nice, neat integer (and also a rational number, since $3 = \frac{3}{1}$). But what about the square root of a number that isn't a perfect square, like 2?

The number $\sqrt{2}$ is a real, physical lengthâ€”it's the diagonal of a square with sides of length 1. However, it has been proven that $\sqrt{2}$ cannot be written as a fraction $\frac{p}{q}$.

Numbers that cannot be expressed as a fraction of two integers are called irrational numbers. Other famous examples include $\pi$ (approximately 3.14159...) and $e$ (approximately 2.71828...).

The Complete Number Line: Real Numbers

So, the number line is not just made of rational numbers. It has gaps, and these gaps are filled by the irrational numbers. When we put all the rational and all the irrational numbers together, we get the set of real numbers, represented by the symbol $\mathbb{R}$.

The real numbers are also dense, just like the rationals. Between any two real numbers, you can always find another one by taking their average.

Hierarchy of Numbers

Every natural number is an integer.

Every integer is a rational number (e.g., $5 = \frac{5}{1}$).

Every rational number is a real number.

What Lies Beyond? Complex Numbers

We found irrational numbers by trying to take the square root of numbers like 2. But what happens if we try to take the square root of a negative number, like $\sqrt{-1}$?

According to the rules of multiplying positive and negative numbers, any real number squared ($r \times r$) must be positive. So, $\sqrt{-1}$ cannot be a real number. To solve this, mathematicians invented a new type of number called a complex number.

Don't worry! While complex numbers are fascinating and important in fields like engineering and physics, we won't need to use them in this course.
